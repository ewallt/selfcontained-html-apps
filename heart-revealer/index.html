The Great Unveiling: Christ Reveals Every Heart

Core Theme: "Thoughts of Many Hearts Revealed"
Scripture & Central Idea

Scriptural Basis: "...that the thoughts of many hearts may be revealed." (Luke 2:35)

Central Idea: In the light of the Saviour's life and death, the true characters and motives of all—from God to Satan to humanity—are exposed.

Ellen G. White
  • "In the light of the Saviour's life, the hearts of all, even from the Creator to the prince of darkness, are revealed.” (Desire of Ages)
  • Simeon’s prophecy frames the cross as the spotlight that exposes every being—from God to Satan to humankind.
  • Application of Simeon's prophecy: "The hearts of the persecutors, the revilers, the murderers, were indeed revealed… Unbelieving Israel took sides with the first great apostate." (EGW Writings)
Sabbath School Lesson Insights
  • The entire conflict, culminating in the final events, serves as an "even greater revelation of God’s goodness and love than had existed prior to Lucifer’s fall." (Further Thought, SS Lesson)
Other Christian Writers

René Girard: The cross "exposes the scapegoat mechanism” and reveals a God who identifies with victims, not persecutors. (I See Satan Fall Like Lightning)

N.T. Wright: The cross reveals God’s love and justice, showing "what the world’s true King is like.” (The Day the Revolution Began)

Greg Boyd: The cross is "the event that most decisively reveals God’s wisdom and power" and love. (ReKnew blog)

God's Character: Misrepresented vs. Revealed
Ellen G. White

Satan's Misrepresentation of God:

  • "Satan has represented God as selfish and oppressive, as claiming all, and giving nothing, as requiring the service of His creatures for His own glory, and making no sacrifice for their good." (Desire of Ages)

God's True Character Revealed by Christ:

  • "But the gift of Christ reveals the Father's heart." (Desire of Ages)
  • "It testifies that the thoughts of God toward us are “thoughts of peace, and not of evil.” (Jeremiah 29:11, quoted in Desire of Ages)
  • "While God's hatred of sin is as strong as death, His love for the sinner is stronger than death." (Desire of Ages)
  • "Having undertaken our redemption, He will spare nothing, however dear, which is necessary to the completion of His work." (Desire of Ages)
  • "No truth essential to our salvation is withheld, no miracle of mercy is neglected, no divine agency is left unemployed. Favor is heaped upon favor, gift upon gift." (Desire of Ages)
  • "The whole treasury of heaven is open to those He seeks to save... Having collected the riches of the universe... He gives them all into the hands of Christ, and says, All these are for man." (Desire of Ages)
  • "Use these gifts to convince him that there is no love greater than Mine in earth or heaven." (Desire of Ages)
  • "By pouring the whole treasury of heaven into this world… God has purchased the will, the affections, the mind, the soul, of every human being.” (Christ’s Object Lessons 326)
  • "All men have been bought with this infinite price.” (God’s Amazing Grace 173)
Sabbath School Lesson Insights
  • Satan sought to "destroy confidence in God’s throne." (Further Thought, SS Lesson)
  • God allowed rebellion to continue to "show the universe the depths of wickedness that come from self-exaltation," thus highlighting His own justice and patience. (Further Thought, SS Lesson)
  • Ultimately, "the goodness of and love of God will be revealed in ways it otherwise never would have been." (Further Thought, SS Lesson)
  • The heavenly host proclaims Christ's worthiness because He was slain and "with your blood you purchased for God, persons from every tribe..." (Revelation 5:9, quoted in SS Lesson) – This reveals God's redemptive character.
Other Christian Writers

Graham Maxwell: “Christ’s death on the cross is the most costly and convincing evidence in favor of the loving character of God.”

Greg Boyd: "The cross is the fullness of God’s love... every portrait of God... must be read through Calvary’s self-giving love."

Satan (Lucifer): His Fall, Motives & Unmasking
Ellen G. White
  • Satan's representation of God as selfish and oppressive actually reflects his own desires and character.
  • He is "the first great apostate." (EGW Writings)
  • His murderous intent was fully revealed at the cross.
Sabbath School Lesson Insights

Original state & Motive for fall:

  • "Lucifer once held the position of covering cherub." (SS Lesson)
  • "He began to consider his own glory, not the glory of his Creator... imagined that he was not being given the deference due to him." (SS Lesson, referencing Ezekiel 28:11-17, Isaiah 14:12-14)

Consequence & Ongoing agenda:

  • "Lucifer was removed from the holy mountain... Lucifer is said to have been in Eden [but was cast out]." (SS Lesson)
  • "Satan... sought to destroy confidence in God’s throne" and "managed to deceive the human race." (Further Thought, SS Lesson)
Other Christian Writers

René Girard: The cross exposes the "scapegoat mechanism," often reflecting a Satanic principle of accusation and violence. (I See Satan Fall Like Lightning)

Christ: The Divine Revealer, Sacrifice & Victor
Ellen G. White
  • "But the gift of Christ reveals the Father's heart." (Desire of Ages)
  • Through Christ, it's testified that God's "thoughts toward us are 'thoughts of peace, and not of evil.'" (Desire of Ages)
  • In undertaking redemption, God, through Christ, "will spare nothing, however dear, which is necessary to the completion of His work." (Desire of Ages)
  • Christ "looks upon the redeemed, renewed in His own image... He beholds in them the result of the travail of His soul, and He is satisfied. Then... He declares, ‘Behold the purchase of My blood! For these I suffered, for these I died...'" (Great Controversy, p. 671)
Sabbath School Lesson Insights
  • "Christ utterly defeated him [Satan] at the cross, securing a place for humanity where fallen angels once stood." (Further Thought, SS Lesson)
  • The redeemed declare Christ worthy because He "was slain, and with your blood you purchased for God, persons from every tribe..." (Revelation 5:9, quoted in SS Lesson)
  • "There is not a human being in all earth’s history for whom Christ has not died." (SS Lesson)
  • The song of the redeemed: "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain..." (Revelation 5:12, quoted in SS Lesson)
Other Christian Writers

N.T. Wright: The cross is Christ's "enthronement and revelation: it shows what the world’s true King is like." (The Day the Revolution Began)

Humanity: Impact, Invitation & Restoration
Ellen G. White
  • The Invitation: "His greatest happiness will be found in loving Me." (Desire of Ages)
  • God's Provision: "The whole treasury of heaven is open to those He seeks to save." (Desire of Ages)
  • Universal Purchase: "All men have been bought with this infinite price." (God’s Amazing Grace 173)
  • Complete Purchase: "God has purchased the will, the affections, the mind, the soul, of every human being.” (Christ’s Object Lessons 326)
  • Glorious Restoration: "The vacancies made in heaven by the fall of Satan and his angels will be filled by the redeemed of the Lord.” (Review & Herald, May 29, 1900)
  • Transformation: The redeemed are "renewed in His own image, every heart bearing the perfect impress of the divine, every face reflecting the likeness of their King." (Great Controversy, p. 671)
Sabbath School Lesson Insights
  • Humanity's destiny contrasted with Satan's: "Humanity is being restored to paradise through Christ," and the redeemed "stand on Mount Zion with the Lamb of God." (SS Lesson)
  • Basis of Restoration: The redeemed "are there, in heaven, only because of the gospel." (SS Lesson)
  • Universal Opportunity: "There is not a human being... for whom Christ has not died. They just need to learn about it and choose to accept it." (SS Lesson)
  • Humanity's Stand: "Sinners, in Christ, publicly turn against Lucifer’s claims." (Further Thought, SS Lesson)
  • Our Call to Action: "What role do we have as a church, and as individuals, in letting people know about what Christ has done for them?" (SS Lesson)
Other Christian Writers

E.J. Waggoner: On the human response to God's revealed grace (referencing Isaiah 6): "‘This hath touched thy lips; and thine iniquity is taken away.’ … One honest confession—and the same fire that cleanses the saint will consume the sin that clings to the impenitent.” (Present Truth, 1899)
