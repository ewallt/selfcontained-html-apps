Life, Death & Resurrection

A Biblical Study Guide on the Core Doctrine

Module 1 – The Core Doctrine

Natural State of Humanity (Mortality)

Humanity is mortal by nature  Gen 2:7 Ps 8:4 Ps 146:4 1 Tim 6:15-16

Death as Unconscious Sleep

The dead know nothing, their thoughts have perished; Jesus likens death to sleep  Eccl 9:5-6, 10 Ps 115:17 John 11:11-14 1 Thess 4:13-15

Two Resurrections

Resurrection "of life" for the righteous, "of damnation" for the lost  John 5:28-29 Dan 12:2 Rev 20:4-6, 11-15

Conditional Immortality

Immortality is granted only to the righteous at Christ's return  Rom 2:7 1 Cor 15:51-54 1 Thess 4:16-17 2 Tim 1:10

Module 3 – Answering Common Questions

Rich Man & Lazarus (Luke 16:19-31)
A parable employing current Jewish imagery; its purpose is moral warning. Literal-sleep texts interpret figurative passages, not vice-versa  Eccl 9:5-6 John 11:11-14
Thief on the Cross (Luke 23:43)
Greek manuscripts had no punctuation; reading, "Truly I say to you today, you will be with Me in Paradise," matches Christ's own burial-to-resurrection timeline  John 20:17 and the corporate resurrection hope  1 Thess 4:16-17
"Eternal Punishment" (Matthew 25:46)
Aiōnios describes the lasting result, not an unending process: "punishment" = "second death"  Rev 20:14-15. Parallel usage in  Jude 7 ("eternal fire" already ashes) shows outcome, not ongoing torment.
"Absent from the Body…present with the Lord" (2 Corinthians 5:8)
Paul contrasts mortal present with resurrected "house from heaven" (vs 1-4); presence with the Lord is realized at the resurrection  2 Tim 4:8

Module 4 – Contrasting Views

Natural Immortality

Core Claim: Soul is inherently immortal, survives death.

Biblical Response: Only God "has immortality" 1 Tim 6:16; immortality is "put on" at resurrection 1 Cor 15:53-54.

Issue Addressed: Undermines need of resurrection.

Eternal Conscious Torment (ECT)

Core Claim: Wicked suffer forever in conscious pain.

Biblical Response: "The wages of sin is death" Rom 6:23; "both soul and body" destroyed Matt 10:28; "second death" ends existence Rev 20:14.

Issue Addressed: Presents God as perpetually torturing.

Module 6 – Glossary of Key Terms

Soul (nephesh / psyche)

The living person as a whole Gen 2:7 Acts 2:41. Not an immortal entity separate from the body.

Spirit (ruach / pneuma)

Breath or life-spark that returns to God at death Eccl 12:7 Luke 23:46.

Hell (Gehenna)

Final place of total destruction for the wicked, named after the Valley of Hinnom Matt 10:28 Mk 9:43-48.

Hades / Sheol

The grave, realm of the dead, a state of silence and unconsciousness Ps 16:10 Rev 1:18.

Second Death

Irreversible extinction of the wicked after the final judgment Rev 20:14-15 Rev 21:8.
