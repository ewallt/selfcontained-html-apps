Faith That Works

An Interactive Theological Exploration

The Central Dilemma

A common pastoral challenge arises from a narrow view of justification by faith. When faith is seen as a purely external, legal declaration that is divorced from a person's inner transformation, it can create a trap—leading to a legalistic, performance-based Christianity. This exploration contrasts that view with an integrated model where faith truly works.

A "Divorced" Justification

Justification is merely a "legal fiction." An external declaration that is separate from inner change.

→

This creates a vacuum, forcing a reliance on human effort for good works, which can lead to legalism and anxiety.

An Integrated Faith

Justification is a transformative event. It is inseparable from the process of being made righteous.

→

Good works flow naturally and joyfully as the organic fruit of a changed heart and a new motivation.

A Unified Solution

Though Waggoner focuses on the *mechanics* of transformation (indwelling) and Wieland on the *motivation* (gratitude), they arrive at the same conclusion. They heal the theological divorce by making justification and sanctification two inseparable facets of a single, transformative event: the union of the believer with the living Christ.
