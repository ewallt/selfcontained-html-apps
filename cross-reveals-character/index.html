Cross Reveals Character in the Cosmic Conflict
Ellen G. White
  • Simeon’s prophecy (Luke 2:35) applies universally: at Calvary the hearts of all—from God to Satan—are laid bare (DA 57).
  • “The whole treasury of heaven” is poured out; God withholds nothing to save humanity, proving His love stronger than death (COL 326).
  • Calvary unmasks Satan’s accusations and calls the church to repentance and mission (PK 314–316).
E. J. Waggoner
  • Isaiah’s live coal shows instant justification by faith: one honest confession and sin is purged (Treasures in Isaiah, 1899).
  • The same cleansing fire will consume sin that is clung to, refuting Satan’s claim that God is unforgiving.
  • Christ’s cross covers the earth with glory; righteousness by faith is God’s answer in the controversy.
A. T. Jones
  • 1893 General Conference sermons link Isaiah 6 & 33: believers must learn to “dwell with everlasting burnings.”
  • The presence that purifies saints is the same fire that will destroy sin when Christ returns.
  • The cross equips the remnant for the loud cry, demonstrating God’s righteous character.
Graham Maxwell
  • Calls Calvary “the most costly and convincing evidence” that God is trustworthy.
  • Only demonstration, not assertion, could settle the cosmic questions about God’s character.
  • Emphasises relational trust: God invites intelligent beings to be friends, not slaves.
Greg Boyd
  • The cross is the definitive revelation of God’s power and wisdom—self‑giving love conquers violence.
  • “Cruciform hermeneutic”: every picture of God must align with the crucified Christ.
  • At Calvary, the principalities are exposed as murderous; God’s true nature is vindicated.
René Girard
  • The cross unmasks the scapegoat mechanism and exposes humanity’s violent lie about the divine.
  • God identifies with victims, not persecutors, revealing a love opposed to sanctioned violence.
  • “I saw Satan fall”: accusations collapse when the innocent victim is vindicated.
N. T. Wright
  • The cross is the enthronement of Israel’s Messiah; it reveals God’s love and justice in action.
  • “The day the revolution began”: self‑giving love launches the kingdom’s victory.
  • Calvary overturns worldly power structures, demonstrating the true nature of divine authority.
